Letter O13 December 1707Leibniz to BouvetMy Very Reverend Father,It has been some time since I received 16 Chinese books, and I do not at all doubt that they are a present which you sent me, for which I am infinitely obliged to you. But as your letter which apparently said what these books were was lost, it is a hidden treasure for me. I wrote to you about them a very long time ago, but I do not know whether the letter was delivered to you in this time of war and disorder.1 Thus, I repeat to you the request to let me know what these books contain. I am now sending you the questions concerning the Jews of China2 which come from Monsieur Jablonski, a very knowledgeable man in Hebrew, who a few years ago gave us a really good edition of the Hebrew bible. He conjectures, based on printed accounts, that there have been Jews living in China for a very long time.
